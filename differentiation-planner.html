Quick Reference — The Four Needs

Grounded in Self-Determination Theory and Maslow's Hierarchy. When students' needs are met, they are intrinsically motivated and behavior improves.

🛡️ Safety & Comfort

  • Physical: body temperature, sensory stimulation, hygiene, pain
  • Emotional: worries, embarrassment, guilt, shame, fear
  • When students feel safe, stress decreases and focus, memory, and willingness to take risks improve
  • Compromised by: sensory overload, trauma, unpredictable environments, loud noises, large groups

🤝 Belonging

  • Positive, mutually satisfying relationships with warmth and trust
  • When students belong, they feel safe to contribute, experiment, make mistakes, and learn
  • Compromised by: unwanted attention, "othering," poor social skills, inhibited communication, cognitive distortions
  • Support with: restorative practices, SEL, community building, inclusive language

🧭 Autonomy

  • People experience higher motivation when they feel control over what they do
  • Promotes intrinsic motivation — engaging for inherent satisfaction
  • Compromised by: poor executive functioning, inability to communicate, low organizational skills
  • Support with: checklists, flow charts, visual schedules, predictable routines, choice boards

🏆 Competency

  • Feelings of effectiveness and confidence in achieving desired outcomes
  • As learners achieve progress and success, motivation and confidence grow
  • Compromised by: learned helplessness, previous failures, reading level, being talked "down" to
  • Support with: strength-based culture, chunking information, scaffolded tasks, feedback loops

Toolkit Categories Overview

Strategies are organized into four categories that work together to support the whole student.

🏫 Organizing the Space / Environment

  • Designated spaces with clear boundaries
  • Materials for differentiation (tactile, visual, auditory)
  • Sensory-friendly adaptations
  • Strategic student seating
  • Student ownership of space
  • Constructivist learning areas

📚 Instruction & Lesson Design

  • Organizing the lesson (repetition, soft starts)
  • Executive functioning supports (checklists, flow charts, visuals)
  • Giving directions (clarity, checks for understanding, prompts)
  • Recall & retention (chunking, mnemonics, key words)
  • Attention-grabbing techniques

💜 Classroom Culture

  • Conscious classroom management
  • Strength-based culture
  • Restorative practices
  • Social emotional learning
  • Joy and community

🔧 Infrastructure & Management

  • Routines and predictability
  • Feedback systems
  • Alternative seating and fidgets
  • Community building
  • Boundaries and triggers awareness
  • Student interests and humor
